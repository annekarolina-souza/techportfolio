A little bit about Anne...

In eighth grade, while participating in my first astronomy olympiad, I discovered a fascination with space. I was captivated by the idea that we could build tools not only to observe the cosmos, but also to see ourselves from beyond our own home. From that moment, I began to consider how my interests in robotics and problem-solving—skills I developed through middle school robotics competitions—could extend beyond Earth. This curiosity led me to study Analysis and Systems Development, where I learned that almost anything can be built, planned, and, most importantly, solved through technology. With this problem-solving mindset and a desire to explore, I chose to pursue aerospace engineering. I am particularly interested in dark matter and its influence on the dynamics of galaxies and interstellar gases, which guides my scientific focus.
I am also passionate about telescope design and instrumentation, essential tools for precise observations of cosmic phenomena, and spacecraft propulsion, which enables exploration beyond Earth. Together, these interests reflect my goal of combining theoretical understanding with practical technologies to advance our knowledge of the universe. I am eager to continue learning, deepen my understanding of astrophysics, explore new technologies, and develop solutions that expand our ability to study the cosmos.

Interests

My main research interest lies in understanding galactic dynamics, the behavior of interstellar gases, and their connection to dark matter, although I am not currently conducting research in this area. I focus on analyzing how these components interact to shape galaxy structures and evolution. My broader scientific interests include telescope instrumentation, image processing techniques, and spacecraft propulsion , with an emphasis on the tools and methods that enable precise observations and experimentation.
